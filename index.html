Stay Informed, Stay Safe

Food allergies can cause serious and sometimes life-threatening reactions. The best way to prevent an allergic reaction is to know your triggers and avoid them. Protect yourself by reading food labels, asking about ingredients, and carrying emergency medication if prescribed.

What is a Food Allergy?

A food allergy is an abnormal immune system reaction to certain foods. Even a tiny amount of the allergy-causing food can trigger symptoms such as hives, swelling, or difficulty breathing. Recognizing food allergies early is important for prevention and safety.

Symptoms of Food Allergy

Food allergy symptoms can range from mild to severe. Common signs include hives, itching, swelling of the lips or tongue, stomach pain, vomiting, or difficulty breathing. Severe reactions may lead to anaphylaxis, a life-threatening condition that requires immediate medical attention.
